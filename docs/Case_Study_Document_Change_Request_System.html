Problem Statement

A high-volume, uncoordinated process

Before implementation, document change requests across multiple projects were impossible to track reliably. Requests arrived through email, SharePoint comments, multiple Monday boards, and verbal requests — with no unified intake, no ownership visibility, and no standardized approval path.

Limited visibility into the status and ownership of active change requests
Inconsistent prioritization across multiple concurrent projects
Back-and-forth clarifications due to incomplete or inconsistent change information
Increased coordination load on a short-staffed QA team
Increased operational risk due to lack of standardized governance
No single source of truth — requests scattered across channels
Solution Architecture

Six integrated stages — end to end

The Monday.com workspace manages the complete document change request lifecycle through six integrated stages designed to enforce structured workflow governance, review accountability, and end-to-end traceability while improving operational efficiency.

Workflow · Stage-by-Stage
1
Stage 1 · Request Form Submission
Standardized Intake via Monday Form
Users submit requests through a standardized Monday Form accessible across all departments. Required metadata includes document type, category, change reason, priority, due date, and attachments — eliminating incomplete or ambiguous requests at source.
Document Type Priority Field Due Date Attachments Conditional Logic
2
Stage 2 · Intake Board + Manager Review
Centralized Manager Decision Queue
All submissions appear in a centralized Intake Board for manager decision-making. Automations notify the requester and route approved items to the correct Project Board.
Approved
Auto-route to project board
Rejected
Notification with justification
Reassigned
Returned for more detail
3
Stage 3 · Departmental Workflow
Stage-Gated Review per Project
Each Project Board follows a controlled workflow aligned to stage gates — ensuring no item advances without completing the prior review step. Items reaching QA Review automatically surface on the central QA dashboard.
Draft Initiated Draft In Progress Department Review QA Review QA Pre-Approved Client Review Final Approved
4
Stage 4 · Central QA Review Board
Cross-Project QA Consolidation Dashboard
When items reach QA Review, they automatically appear on a centralized QA dashboard consolidating requests from all projects. QA can drag-and-drop prioritize while preserving metadata, comments, and attachments — giving a short-staffed team full control without losing traceability.
Cross-project consolidation Drag-drop prioritization Metadata preserved Attachment access
5
Stage 5 · QA Pre-Approved → Project Board Return
Return to Project for Final Closure
After QA pre-approval, items automatically return to the originating Project Board for final stakeholder steps (if applicable) and final approval — keeping the QA board clean and the project board as the single source of record.
6
Stage 6 · Effective Documents Repository
Final Archive & Traceability
Once marked Effective, items move to the Effective Documents Board — the final archive. Active boards stay clean while complete change history, metadata, and approval records are preserved for reporting, audit support, and inspection readiness.
Audit-ready Complete history Inspection support GMP traceability
